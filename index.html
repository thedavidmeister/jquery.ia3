Hello designers

It is time to end the discussion and start blaming the developer,
because now you can easilly point out he did a bad job.

Image-A-Trois helps you find the differences between
a design and the actuall webpage.

Try it yourself

The Image-A-Trois overlay on the right has been set up to contain the graphic design of this very page (with alternate colors for a better perception).

Try dragging and resizing the overlay, zoom in, scroll or resize your browser. Image-A-Trois-doesn't care.

Bored of just dragging and resizing?
Try the advanced options as explained below.

Advanced options

  1. Hover over the overlay and click the pin-icon to pin it to the page.
  2. Click and drag the bottom part of the overlay to set its opacity.

How it works

Image-A-Trois allows you to compare a webpage agains up to three images (probably an image of the graphic design), just by clicking a bookmarklet.

Why three images? Well, at first it could handle only one image, but "Image-A-Un" just didn't sound that catchy.
It is limited to three for that same reason.

Test your developer in only four steps

  • Install the bookmarklet, visit a webpage and click it;
  • Specify how the page is aligned (left or centered);
  • Select the graphic design (any image will do);
  • Drag, scroll and find the differences.

Install it, NOW!

Installing Image-A-Trois couldn't be easier,
just drag this button to the bookmarks bar.
Done.
